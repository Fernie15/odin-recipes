Tonkatsu Ramen (とんこつラーメン)

Description

Tonkatsu ramen is a popular Japanese noodle cuisine that perfectly combines tonkatsu broth and ramen noodles. Tonkatsu broth is a creamy pork-based soup. This is made by simmering pork bones for several hours until the broth becomes thick. Ramen noodles are used for the tonkatsu ramen are normally thin and springy. They are served inside of the tonkatsu broth.

To accompany this amazing dish, you can add a variety of toppings such as Chashu (チャーシュー) which are thin slices of pork belly, boiled eggs are also an excellent addition to this dish, as well as many other great toppings.

Ingredients

  • 1 portion ramen noodles frozen or instant
  • Tonkatsu broth (premade)
  • 3-4 tbsp tsuyu(Japanese soup base sause) or chashu tare
  • 1 chopped green onion
  • 1 green onion
  • Handful mushrooms (your preferance)
  • 1-2 slices of chashu
  • 1 egg
  • 1 clove garlic

Steps

  1. In a medium pot bring the tonkatsu broth to a simmer over medium heat
  2. In another medium pot add water and bring to a boil
  3. While those are heating up, we can prepare the toppings. Chop green onions and slice mushrooms.
  4. Prepare the chashu by searing a dry pan over high heat, torch or high broil for 3-5 minutes
  5. Now that the ramen water is boiling add the white part of the green onions to the water, the mushrooms, and the ramen noodles. Fresh or frozen noodles need about 2 minutes to cook and instant ramen will take about 3-4 minutes.
  6. Once the noodles are done, drain them well and add to a bown. Stir noodles to coat evenly with the broth.
  7. Add your toppings to the bowl including the green part of the green onion, the boiled egg, the cooked chashu, and optional you can add sesame seeds and garlic to your taste
  8. Lastly enjoy your bowl of tonkatsu ramen!
